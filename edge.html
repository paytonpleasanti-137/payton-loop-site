What is "The Edge"?

We are currently in one of those windows. The people who understand these now — before regulation standardizes and compresses returns — have the asymmetric advantage.

Liquidity Provision — Earn Fees from Every Trade
Passive income · Impermanent loss risk · Best for stablecoin pairs
Unregulated Higher Risk
▼
Decentralized exchanges (Uniswap, Curve) let you deposit two assets into a liquidity pool. Every time someone trades through that pool, you earn a fee (0.01–1% per trade). The risk is "impermanent loss" — if the prices of your two assets diverge significantly, you end up holding less value than if you'd just held them. The solution: only provide liquidity for stablecoin-to-stablecoin pairs (USDC/USDT) where both assets are always ~$1.

Arbitrage is the simultaneous buying and selling of the same asset in different markets to profit from a price difference. In theory it's risk-free. In practice, execution speed and fees determine whether you actually capture the spread. Three types matter for Payton Loop: prediction market arb, sports book arb, and onchain DEX arb.
Sports Book Arbitrage — Guaranteed Profit on Both Sides
100% Legal in Oklahoma · 2–8% per arb · Account banning risk
