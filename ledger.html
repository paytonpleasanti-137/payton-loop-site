About This Ledger

What is a General Ledger & Why Does It Matter?

I.
The oldest financial technology. Double-entry bookkeeping was invented in 1494 by Luca Pacioli — the same mathematics underlying every Fortune 500 company, every bank, and every investment portfolio today. For every transaction, something is debited (goes out or increases an asset) and something is credited (comes in or creates a liability). The books always balance.
II.
Why Buffett reads ledgers, not headlines. Warren Buffett has said he can evaluate a business in under an hour if he has the right financial statements. A ledger tells you: where does money come from? Where does it go? Is the business generating cash or consuming it? Every entry in this ledger is an answer to those questions for Payton Loop.
III.
Debits and Credits — the rule. A Debit increases assets or expenses (money leaves the account or you spend it). A Credit increases revenue or liabilities (money enters or you owe it). In this ledger: Debits = money deployed (expenses, investments). Credits = money earned (sales, affiliate commissions). Net = your position.
IV.
Why this matters for Payton Loop. Without a ledger, you can't answer: Am I profitable? What's my ROI? When do I break even? What's my burn rate? With this ledger, those answers are always current, always visible, always honest. This is how serious operators think — not in impressions or follower counts, but in entries, balances, and returns.
Ledger Principle: All entries are permanent. Errors are corrected by posting a new offsetting entry — never by deleting. This creates an immutable audit trail. Every entry you post here is timestamped and stored in your browser's localStorage. Export your ledger at any time using the button below.
