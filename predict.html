Edge Analyzer tab · Clean Ticket Protocol
Run the Edge Analyzer on any Kalshi market. If your true probability estimate is more than 5 percentage points better than the market price, you have positive expected value. Only bet when the math says yes.
Step 3: Size correctly using Kelly Criterion
Quarter-Kelly · Max 2% of bankroll per bet
Kelly Criterion tells you the mathematically optimal bet size. Always use Quarter-Kelly (25% of full Kelly) to account for probability estimation error. Never bet more than 2% of your total bankroll on a single contract.

When the same question trades at different prices on Polymarket vs Kalshi, you can bet both sides and lock in a risk-free profit. This is called arbitrage. The scanner below identifies structural arb opportunities.

How Prediction Market Arb Works
Example: Structural Arb
Polymarket: "Fed cuts in May" — YES at 42¢ Kalshi: "Fed cuts in May" — NO at 52¢ Buy YES on Polymarket for $42 → pays $100 if yes Buy NO on Kalshi for $52 → pays $100 if no Total cost: $94 Guaranteed payout: $100 (one side always wins) Risk-free profit: $6 on $94 = 6.4% return
This only works when the two markets price the SAME event. The spread disappears quickly as arbitrageurs trade it. Speed matters — set up accounts on both platforms first.
